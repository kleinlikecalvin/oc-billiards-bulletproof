The Do's and Don'ts of league play

The Do's

Let’s take a minute to talk about some do’s and don’t about pool leagues play.

  • Ask your local bar or pool hall if they offer leagues and which ones.
  • Form a team with a group of your friends. Most leagues will place you on a team if you don’t have enough friends to form your own team but it is always more fun to play on a team with people you know.
  • Choose a league that suits you and your ability. There are many types of leagues and we will discuss some of those later.
  • Go out to a league match and watch them play before joining to make sure you know what to expect before diving in to the deep end of the “pool.”

The Don'ts

Now for some things you shouldn’t do or worry about when considering league play.

  • Worry that you are not good enough. There are many league that want and need players of every skill level. All you have to do is find the one that is right for you.
  • Be concerned that people will laugh at you if you don’t play well. Even the best player in the world remembers when he or she played just as bad as you! Besides you’ll probably be having so much fun laughing at yourself that you won’t care what that a$$H@le on the barstool is doing!
  • Expect to win every match. Just have fun.
  • Get mad if someone shows poor sportsmanship. Remember…you’re playing pool in a bar! It’s gonna happen…there are sore losers out there. Not like bigfoot or the Loch Ness monster…Really! I have seen real live sore losers with my own eyes and many of them play pool in bars and join pool leagues!
  • Hesitate to ask questions. There are many resources online that can help you decide which leagues offer what and if you’re reading this you probably already know someone that plays in a league.
